## Introduction

A Virtual Private Network can be useful when connecting from public Wi-Fi, reducing exposure to local network monitoring, or adding a basic privacy layer during everyday browsing.

A VPN should not be treated as a complete anonymity solution. It does not replace safe browsing habits, endpoint security, account protection, or proper operational security. For sensitive investigations, journalism, or threat intelligence activities, VPN usage should be part of a broader security model.

This list focuses on reputable free or freemium VPN services with reasonable privacy practices and clear limitations.

---

## Proton VPN

**Website:** [https://protonvpn.com](https://protonvpn.com)  
**Origin:** Switzerland  
**Free plan:** Yes, with unlimited data

### Strengths

- Strong privacy-oriented positioning
- Unlimited data on the free plan
- Open-source applications
- No advertising-based business model
- Suitable for always-on basic protection

### Limitations

- Limited server choice on the free plan
- Lower priority compared with paid users
- Advanced features require a paid subscription

---

## Windscribe

**Website:** [https://windscribe.com](https://windscribe.com)  
**Free plan:** Yes, with a monthly data allowance

### Strengths

- Generous free data allowance after email confirmation
- Support for modern VPN protocols
- Browser extension available
- Includes privacy-oriented features such as tracker and ad blocking

### Limitations

- Data cap on the free plan
- Limited server locations compared with paid plans
- Some features require configuration awareness

## Services to Avoid

Be cautious with unknown VPN services that promise unlimited free access without a clear business model. Many low-quality free VPNs monetize users through aggressive advertising, tracking, traffic redirection, or data collection.

As a rule, avoid services that:

- do not clearly explain their privacy policy;
- have no transparent company information;
- require unnecessary permissions;
- inject ads or modify browser traffic;
- make unrealistic claims about full anonymity.

---

## Practical Recommendations

For basic privacy, prefer reputable freemium services such as Proton VPN, Windscribe, PrivadoVPN, or TunnelBear, depending on your use case.

For stronger protection, combine VPN usage with:

- secure DNS configuration;
- HTTPS-only mode in the browser;
- multi-factor authentication on important accounts;
- updated operating systems and browsers;
- ad and tracker blocking extensions from trusted sources.

---

## Conclusion

Free VPN services can be useful for occasional protection, testing, or low-risk browsing, but they should be selected carefully. The best choice is usually a transparent freemium provider with a sustainable business model, clear limitations, and a documented privacy posture.

For professional or high-risk use cases, a VPN should be only one layer in a broader security architecture.
